FEDERAL INCOME TAX BRACKETS — TAX YEAR 2024

HOW BRACKETS WORK

The U.S. uses a progressive marginal tax system. Each bracket rate applies only to income within that bracket, not to your total income. Your "effective tax rate" is your total tax divided by total taxable income and will be lower than your marginal rate.

CALCULATING YOUR TAX (EXAMPLE)

A single filer with $80,000 of taxable income pays:
- 10% on $11,600 = $1,160
- 12% on ($47,150 – $11,600) = $4,266
- 22% on ($80,000 – $47,150) = $7,227
Total tax: $12,653 | Effective rate: 15.8% | Marginal rate: 22%

KIDDIE TAX

Unearned income (dividends, capital gains) of children under 19 (or under 24 if full-time students) above $2,500 is taxed at the parent's marginal rate.

ALTERNATIVE MINIMUM TAX (AMT)

Certain deductions and exclusions are "preference items" that must be added back when computing AMT. The AMT exemption for 2024:
- Single: $85,700 (phases out above $609,350)
- Married Filing Jointly: $133,300 (phases out above $1,218,700)
AMT rates: 26% on the first $220,700 of AMT income; 28% above that.

TAX CREDITS vs. DEDUCTIONS

- Deductions reduce taxable income (value = deduction × marginal rate)
- Credits reduce tax liability dollar-for-dollar

Key 2024 credits:
- Child Tax Credit: $2,000 per qualifying child under 17; $1,700 refundable portion
- Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC): Up to $7,830 for families with 3+ children
- Child and Dependent Care Credit: Up to 35% of $3,000 (one dependent) or $6,000 (two+)
- American Opportunity Credit: Up to $2,500 per student for first four years of higher education
- Lifetime Learning Credit: Up to $2,000 per return (20% of first $10,000 of qualified expenses)
