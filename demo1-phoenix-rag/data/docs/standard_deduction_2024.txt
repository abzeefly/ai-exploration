STANDARD DEDUCTION AND ITEMIZED DEDUCTIONS — TAX YEAR 2024

STANDARD DEDUCTION AMOUNTS

For tax year 2024, the standard deduction amounts are:
- Single or Married Filing Separately: $14,600
- Married Filing Jointly or Qualifying Surviving Spouse: $29,200
- Head of Household: $21,900

Additional Standard Deduction for Age 65 or Older / Blindness:
- Single or Head of Household (per qualifying condition): $1,950
- Married Filing Jointly (per qualifying condition): $1,550

WHEN TO ITEMIZE

You should itemize deductions if your total itemizable expenses exceed the standard deduction for your filing status. Common itemized deductions include:

1. State and Local Taxes (SALT): Deductible up to $10,000 combined for state and local income taxes (or sales taxes) and property taxes.

2. Mortgage Interest: Interest on loans secured by your primary or secondary residence is deductible on up to $750,000 of acquisition debt (for loans originated after December 15, 2017).

3. Charitable Contributions: Cash donations to qualified organizations are deductible up to 60% of your Adjusted Gross Income (AGI). Non-cash donations are generally limited to 30% of AGI.

4. Medical and Dental Expenses: You can deduct unreimbursed medical expenses exceeding 7.5% of your AGI.

5. Casualty and Theft Losses: Only losses from federally declared disasters are deductible in 2024.

INTERACTION WITH AMT

High-income taxpayers who itemize may be subject to the Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT). The AMT exemption for 2024 is $85,700 for single filers and $133,300 for married filing jointly. SALT deductions are not allowed under AMT.

RECORD-KEEPING

Maintain receipts, bank statements, and acknowledgment letters from charitable organizations for all items you plan to deduct. The IRS may request documentation during an audit.
