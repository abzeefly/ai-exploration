RETIREMENT ACCOUNT CONTRIBUTION LIMITS AND TAX RULES — TAX YEAR 2024

401(k), 403(b), AND 457(b) PLANS

Employee Contribution Limit: $23,000
Catch-Up Contribution (age 50 or older): Additional $7,500 (total $30,500)

Employer contributions (matching, profit-sharing) do not count toward the employee limit but are subject to a combined limit of $69,000 (or $76,500 with catch-up).

TRADITIONAL IRA

Contribution Limit: $7,000 per person ($8,000 if age 50 or older)

Deductibility phase-out ranges (if covered by workplace plan):
- Single/Head of Household: $77,000 – $87,000 MAGI
- Married Filing Jointly: $123,000 – $143,000 MAGI
- Married Filing Separately: $0 – $10,000 MAGI

If not covered by a workplace plan but spouse is covered:
- Married Filing Jointly: $230,000 – $240,000 MAGI

Contributions to Traditional IRA are tax-deferred; distributions in retirement are taxed as ordinary income. Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) begin at age 73.

ROTH IRA

Contribution Limit: Same as Traditional IRA ($7,000 / $8,000 catch-up)

Income phase-out ranges for contributions:
- Single/Head of Household: $146,000 – $161,000 MAGI
- Married Filing Jointly: $230,000 – $240,000 MAGI
- Married Filing Separately: $0 – $10,000 MAGI

Contributions are made with after-tax dollars. Qualified distributions (age 59½ and account at least 5 years old) are tax-free. No RMDs during the original owner's lifetime.

BACKDOOR ROTH IRA

High-income earners who exceed Roth IRA income limits may contribute to a Traditional IRA (non-deductible) and immediately convert to a Roth IRA. The pro-rata rule applies if you have pre-tax IRA balances.

SELF-EMPLOYED RETIREMENT ACCOUNTS

SEP-IRA: Contribute up to 25% of net self-employment income, maximum $69,000 for 2024.

SIMPLE IRA: Employee contribution limit $16,000 ($19,500 with catch-up for age 50+). Employers must either match up to 3% of compensation or contribute 2% for all eligible employees.

Solo 401(k): Combines employee contribution ($23,000 / $30,500 with catch-up) plus employer contribution of up to 25% of compensation, maximum combined $69,000 / $76,500.

EARLY WITHDRAWAL PENALTY

Distributions before age 59½ are generally subject to a 10% early withdrawal penalty in addition to ordinary income tax. Exceptions include:
- Substantially equal periodic payments (72(t) distributions)
- Disability
- First-time home purchase (IRA only, up to $10,000 lifetime)
- Higher education expenses (IRA only)
- Unreimbursed medical expenses exceeding 7.5% of AGI
